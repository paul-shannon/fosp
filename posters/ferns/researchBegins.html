Research Begins

Research started in 2016, as WSU biologists tested for the presence of Phytophthora species. We collected samples. Experiments took place at the WSU Puyallup Research Center. Phytophthora was eliminated as a cause.

In 2018 we ran an ad hoc "community science" experiment, tentatively establishing that a water-borne pathogen may be involved. The experiment is explained in the long version of the film.

Despite the wide spread of the sword-fern die-off across the Puget Lowlands; and the crucial role of sword ferns in the forest understory's ecology, no governmental or academic institutions have, until now, joined in the research.

And even now, funds are in short supply.

The Friends have received enough money, from donations, for a pilot sequencing project. If results are promising, we will scale up the work, collecting many more samples from more locations, in order to identify the cause with statistical rigor.

To that end, the Friends of Seaward Park are now working with WSU plant pathologists, an ecologist and an environmental DNA scientist, in coordination with Seattle Parks, to perform "endophyte DNA sequencing".
