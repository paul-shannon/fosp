A dramatic die-off of the long-lived and robust, drought-tolerant and dominant understory sword fern of PNW lowland douglas-fir forests was first reported in 2014 in an old-growth urban forest in Seattle.

It has since been observed throughout the Puget Lowlands. Sword ferns require mineralized soil and open sunlight to revegetate, so within mature forests with intact canopies, sword fern regeneration is rare. In addition, natural regeneration of other species is very slow: ten years have passed at the original Ground Zero diep0ff site, with nearly zero natural regeneration. We worry that long-term bare ground raises the dangers of cascading ecosystem decline in this already stressed, rare and beautiful old-growth urban forests.
